CIT5900 – Project 3 Research Output

Project Overview: This project, conducted by Group 4 (Victoria Hannett, Ruoxi Wang, Swas Tripathi, and Alan Gu), consolidates the results of all group searches to create a comprehensive inventory of research outputs related to the Federal Statistical Research Data Centers (FSRDCs). The primary goal is to generate a streamlined dataset of these outputs and explore patterns or relationships within the data, such as publication trends, institutional contributions, and thematic focuses. This landing page serves as a public-facing, open-source visualization dashboard, hosted via GitHub Pages, to present key findings through a series of graphs and brief analytical discussions.

📈 Publications Per Year

Description: This line chart with markers illustrates annual publication counts associated with FSRDC research outputs. A distinct green line sets it apart visually, and the rotated x-axis labels ensure legibility across years.

📌 Key Insight:

A two-phase trajectory is evident: publication volume held steady (≤ 5/year) through 2009, but skyrocketed post-2015 — surpassing 120 by 2023. This inflection point mirrors the momentum of open-data policies in federal research, marking 2015–2023 as the most analytically rich window for collaboration network modeling.

📘 Distribution of Output Types

Description: A bar chart with pastel palette shows the distribution of output types. Each bar uses a distinct color to clarify counts without suggesting a hierarchical order.

📌 Key Insight:

Working papers (WP), journal articles (JA), and book chapters (BC) together make up over 90% of all outputs. Less common formats like conference proceedings and technical reports appear infrequently. This highlights the WP/JA/BC trio as the backbone of citable content — essential for any citation or impact-focused analysis.

🏛️ Top 10 RDCs (Research Domain Categories)

Description: A horizontal bar chart sorted in ascending order. This layout emphasizes length of RDC names and draws the eye upward to the most prolific centers using a consistent “Blues_d” palette.

📌 Key Insight:

The top three RDCs account for roughly 40% of all research outputs, while the bottom half of this top-10 list barely surpasses single digits. This suggests a highly centralized research ecosystem where a few hubs dominate FSRDC activity — crucial for prioritizing targets in impact or network analysis.

👩‍🔬 Top 10 Prolific Authors

Description: A horizontal bar chart uses the “Magma” gradient to emphasize rank. Author names are flipped from “Last, F.” to “F. Last” for natural reading.

📌 Key Insight:

A few “power users” dominate the author landscape — the top author contributes 18 papers. This steep drop-off underscores the importance of setting an author threshold (e.g., ≥ 3 papers) when analyzing co-authorship networks, to focus on consistent contributors over incidental ones.

🔠 Top 10 Words in Output Titles

Description: A standard vertical bar chart showing the most frequent words found in output titles. X-axis labels are tilted for clarity, with bars sorted by frequency in descending order.

📌 Key Insight:

The dominance of words like “evidence,” “study,” and “analysis” reveals a strong methodological focus in FSRDC research. The inclusion of terms such as “health,” “data,” and “cancer” highlights public health and epidemiology as core domains. Meanwhile, the presence of “review” suggests a substantial portion of outputs synthesize existing knowledge rather than solely present new findings.
